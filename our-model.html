How It Works

A model built for permanent results

Our approach is simple: house people fast, support them deeply, and measure success by one metric — zero returns to homelessness.

The Four Phases

From Street to Permanent Home

1
Phase One

Street Outreach & Engagement

We meet people where they are — physically and emotionally. Before anything else, we build trust. Our street-outreach team works in high-need urban corridors throughout Los Angeles to identify individuals experiencing homelessness and begin the relationship that makes everything else possible.

  • Consistent presence in identified high-need corridors
  • Trauma-informed, non-judgmental engagement
  • Needs assessment — health, history, barriers to housing
  • Connection to immediate basic needs while trust builds
2
Phase Two

Rapid Rehousing

Speed matters. The longer someone remains unhoused, the harder it becomes to exit homelessness permanently. We cut through bureaucratic delays to place individuals into stable, permanent housing as quickly as possible — without prerequisites, waiting lists, or compliance requirements.

  • No sobriety, employment, or program-compliance requirements
  • Direct placement into permanent housing units
  • Financial assistance for deposits and initial rent
  • Coordination with landlords and property partners
3
Phase Three

Intensive Personalized Support

Housing alone isn't enough. Once housed, each person receives intensive, individualized case management tailored to their specific needs. We work alongside clinical partners to address the full spectrum of challenges that led to — and could lead back to — homelessness.

  • Dedicated case manager for every individual
  • Mental health and substance use support (not a prerequisite — a resource)
  • Employment readiness, benefits enrollment, and life skills
  • Healthcare connection and coordination
  • Community and social support building
4
Phase Four

Long-Term Stability & Follow-Up

Our responsibility doesn't end at move-in. We maintain an ongoing relationship with every individual we house, providing check-ins and responsive support for as long as it takes to ensure permanent stability. Zero returns to homelessness is not a goal — it's our standard.

  • Regular scheduled check-ins for minimum 24 months
  • Rapid response to emerging crises before they escalate
  • Continued access to case management as needed
  • Outcome tracking and data collection for program improvement

The Evidence

Why Housing-First Works

Decades of research consistently show that providing stable housing first — before requiring sobriety, employment, or treatment compliance — leads to dramatically better outcomes for individuals experiencing chronic homelessness.


Treating housing as a right rather than a reward removes the barriers that keep people on the street and creates the stable foundation from which every other area of life can improve.


Project I For One is built entirely on this evidence base. We don't believe in reinventing the wheel — we believe in applying what works, rigorously, at scale.
